# Gas Giant Formation Catalog

The visible cloud formations observed on Jupiter and Saturn, and how each is
implemented in the simulator. This catalog drove the design and doubles as
the acceptance checklist for the simulation core.

**Technique legend**
- **VEL** — a feature of the constructed velocity field (streamfunction ψ)
- **STAMP** — a tracer pattern applied at init and/or regenerated by the
  relaxation forcing
- **EMERGE** — emerges from advecting tracers through the flow
- **DETAIL** — synthesized at derive/export time
- **POST** — applied in map derivation (color/height post-processing)
- **POLAR** — implemented in the polar patch domains

The tracer state is four scalars in one RGBA field: **T0** color index,
**T1** cloud-top height, **T2** detail/freshness, **T3** storm tint
(signed: + warm/red, − blue-gray).

---

## Zonal structure

### Zones (bright bands)
Cream/white high-albedo bands of elevated ammonia cloud; smooth and hazy with
anticyclonic shear. *Implementation:* STAMP — bright values in T0 and high T1
from the seeded band layout (`sim/bands.py`, jittered equal-area spacing);
the relaxation forcing (`advect.comp` pass 2) continually regenerates them,
which is what keeps hue separation alive over arbitrarily long runs.

### Belts (dark bands)
Brown/red-brown darker bands; deeper cloud decks, visibly more turbulent,
cyclonic shear. *Implementation:* STAMP — dark T0, low T1; VEL — the
turbulence amplitude is boosted inside the belt mask (`psi.comp`,
`turbulence.belt_boost`), reproducing their churned look.

### Alternating zonal jets
East–west jet streams with maxima at the zone/belt boundaries; Jupiter has
~30, plus equatorial superrotation (~100–150 m/s; Saturn's equatorial jet is
far broader and faster). All visible streaking is material sheared by these
jets. *Implementation:* VEL backbone — an exact 1D profile built on the CPU
(`sim/profiles.py`): one alternating Gaussian jet per band boundary with
seeded amplitudes and poleward decay, plus the equatorial jet
(`jets.equatorial_speed/width`); ψ is integrated numerically (trapezoid) with
the spherical pairing `u = −∂ψ/∂φ`, `v = (1/cosφ)∂ψ/∂λ`, the only pairing
that is divergence-free on a sphere. Streaking EMERGEs.

### Band-boundary meander
Real band edges are not parallel circles — they wander at planetary
wavenumbers 5–20. Straight band edges are the single biggest "procedural
planet" tell. *Implementation:* VEL+STAMP — a seeded low-frequency sphere-
noise latitude displacement δ(λ,φ) warps both the jet profile lookup and the
band stamps (`bands.warp_amount/warp_freq`).

## Discrete storms (the vortex registry)

All storms live in the velocity field as Gaussian-streamfunction vortices by
great-circle distance (`sim/vortices.py`): coherent rotating cores that
compose freely in ψ and work identically at any latitude including the poles.
Their cloud signature is stamped into the tracers AND included in the
relaxation target at their current (drifting) position — anticyclones
continually regenerate bright cloud tops; without that they dissolve within
~τ steps.

### GRS-class hero anticyclones
A giant salmon/red oval (GRS ~14,000 km at 22°S) with internal spiral,
bright collar, deflected surrounding flow, and a turbulent wake.
*Implementation:* VEL — one to three large vortices (`storms.hero_*`) with a
red T3 core stamp; collar ring and internal spiral EMERGE from rotation.

### Turbulent wakes
Chaotic folded bright filaments trailing downstream of the big storms (the
classic GRS wake). *Implementation:* VEL — a turbulence-amplification wedge
attached downstream of hero vortices (downstream = local jet direction) in
`psi.comp` (`storms.wake_turbulence`); the folded filaments EMERGE.

### White ovals
Compact bright anticyclones (the Oval BA lineage), often in same-latitude
lines, sometimes dark-rimmed. *Implementation:* VEL — medium vortices in
zones, rotation sign matched to the ambient shear (that is what lets them
persist), latitude-conditioned placement, power-law sizes, Poisson-disc
longitude spacing; STAMP — bright disc + darker collar annulus.

### Brown barges
Dark cigar-shaped cyclones, classically in Jupiter's NEB.
*Implementation:* VEL — weak vortices in belts with rotation sign OPPOSED to
the ambient shear; the mismatch lets the jets stretch them into dark
elongated cigars (EMERGE); STAMP — dark T0, warm T3.

### Vortex street / string of pearls
A chain of 6–9 similar small white ovals evenly spaced around one latitude
circle (~40°S on Jupiter, imaged by Juno). *Implementation:* VEL+STAMP —
`storms.pearls_count` same-sign anticyclones at jittered equal spacing on one
seeded zone latitude.

### Anticyclone mergers
Same-sign ovals occasionally orbit each other and merge (BC + DE → BE → BA).
*Deferred to the animation release* — primarily a time-domain phenomenon;
the registry design (drifting vortex objects) is ready for it.

## Waves and instabilities

### Kelvin–Helmholtz billows
Scalloped wave curls along high-shear band boundaries. *Implementation:*
VEL — a sinusoidal ψ perturbation weighted by shear² at the jet flanks
(`turbulence.kh_amplitude/kh_wavenumber`); roll-up EMERGEs.

### Festoons
Blue-gray streamers hooking from the equatorial belt edge across the bright
Equatorial Zone, quasi-periodic (Jupiter's NEB south edge).
*Implementation:* VEL — a Rossby-wave ψ meander on the band edge nearest
~7° (`waves.festoon_strength/festoon_wavenumber`); STAMP — negative-T3
plumes rooted at the wave crests, continually regenerated and sheared into
the characteristic hooks (`wave_stamp.glsl`).

### 5-µm hot spots
Compact cloud-free holes between festoons at the wave troughs — the deepest
visible levels. *Implementation:* STAMP — sharp T1/T0 depressions at the
troughs (`waves.hotspot_depth`); these drive the (future) emission map.

### Saturn ribbon wave
A thin dark wavy ribbon meandering along one mid-latitude jet (~47°N).
*Implementation:* VEL — a standing meander on the strongest mid-latitude
jet; STAMP — a thin dark T0 line that the meander folds into the wave shape
(`waves.ribbon_strength`).

### Mesoscale gravity-wave trains
Fine parallel ripple trains, mostly visible in relief near the equator.
*Implementation:* DETAIL/POST (subtle height ripples) — scheduled with the
animation/emission work; low visual priority at planet scale.

## Convection and texture

### Convective outbreaks (Great White Spot class)
A brilliant white plume erupts and is sheared into a planet-girdling
turbulent collar over weeks (Saturn 2010; Jupiter NTB plumes).
*Implementation:* EVENT (`sim/events.py`) — at a seeded step a bright
zero-circulation stamp (brightness decays over ~160 steps) plus a brief
divergent outflow impulse in the velocity (the one intentional departure
from solenoidal flow); the shear-out EMERGEs. `storms.outbreak_count`.

### Folded filamentary regions
Chaotic folded bright/dark filament fields filling the cyclonic belts.
*Implementation:* EMERGE — belt-boosted turbulence under MacCormack
advection (which keeps fronts crisp where plain semi-Lagrangian would smear
them away).

### Fine filaments / scooter clouds
Wispy streaks riding the jets, visible at high resolution.
*Implementation:* T2 detail tracer (replenished each step ≈ an
exponential-decay flow noise) + DETAIL — export-time advected-coordinate
noise: positions are backtraced through the baked velocity for 2–4 staggered
pseudo-times and high-frequency sphere noise is sampled there, yielding
noise stretched and folded by the flow (true filaments, not line-integral
smear), masked by T2/shear/speed (`render/detail.py`).

### Convective cell fields
Closed-cell / "popcorn" granulation in quiet zone interiors — without it,
quiet regions read as fuzz. *Implementation:* DETAIL — 3D Worley cellular
texture weighted into low-shear, low-speed zone interiors
(`detail.cellular_amount`).

## Polar formations (the patch domains)

The poles are simulated in two square azimuthal-equidistant patches (uniform
resolution across the pole, no singularity), slaved to the equirect grid by a
per-step nesting exchange and composited with a narrow feather.

### Jupiter's polar cyclone clusters
A central polar cyclone ringed by 8 (north) / 5 (south) circumpolar cyclones
at polygon vertices, with fluffy spiral arms (Juno).
*Implementation:* POLAR — registry vortices (great-circle distance works at
the pole by construction): one central + `poles.*.cyclone_count` ring
cyclones; spiral arms EMERGE in the patch sim.

### Saturn's polar hexagon
A hexagonal standing jet wave around a tight polar vortex (78°N).
*Implementation:* POLAR — a ψ jet ridge along
`ρ₀(1 + ε·cos(kθ + φ₀))` with `k = poles.*.polygon_sides` (any polygon), plus
a dark ring stamp that the jet advects into the polygonal shape.

### Plain polar vortex
A single tight polar vortex (a generic default for the south pole).
*Implementation:* POLAR — one larger central registry vortex.

## Global appearance

### Saturn-style global haze
Saturn's muted, butterscotch, low-contrast look — band edges softened,
storms mostly hidden — versus Jupiter's vivid contrast. *Implementation:*
POST — the `appearance.haze_amount` knob blends color toward a haze tint,
compresses contrast and saturation, and flattens height relief. This single
axis is the main Jupiter ↔ Saturn slider.

### Vortex rims / collars
Bright halos or dark rings around the large ovals. *Implementation:* STAMP —
a two-stop radial profile per vortex (core + annulus); rotation sharpens the
ring (EMERGE).
